True to the End

You couldn't invent a better life for a dog. For almost 13 years, Mollie lived in the country with daily walks on the beach. Her attendants, by her side every day, responded to even the sublest signal of need. They were well-trained to open doors, throw balls, supply Greenies, dispense medication, offer beauty treatments, and concoct only the best and most expensive dinners available.

The last weeks of Mollie's life were spent living right on the beach in San Diego. The weather was fine and the walking sand abundant. Her final year, Mollie had been hindered only slightly by her age. She was slow and stiff to rise sometimes. But toss her a ball and she lost 10 years. And most strangers pegged her as an adolescent.

Mollie continued to be active and youthful right up to the end. Just one week before she died, her moms noticed she was walking more slowly and took her to the vet. That's when she was diagnosed with advanced cancer. Her last week was full and happy. She was fetching sticks right up to the day she died. She went bravely, peacefully, and in the arms of her best friends.
